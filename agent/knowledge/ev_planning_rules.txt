1. Charger Expansion: If a station consistently operates with high load during peak hours (risk level 'High'), consider deploying additional Level 3 DC Fast Chargers at that location.
2. Load Balancing Strategy: If peak demand correlates negatively with grid stability (correlation < -0.3), implement a dynamic pricing model to shift demand to off-peak hours instead of immediately expanding chargers.
3. Grid Buffer Zones: In areas where repeated congestion leads to grid instability, battery energy storage systems (BESS) should be installed alongside chargers to act as a buffer.
4. Smart Scheduling: High-load periods between 17:00 and 21:00 require proactive scheduling via mobile apps. Incentive programs (rebates) must be deployed for users charging after 22:00.
